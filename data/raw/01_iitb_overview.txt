IIT Bombay — Institute Overview
Source: Wikipedia, "IIT Bombay" (general reference; supplement with official IIT Bombay PDFs)

Founding and status
The Indian Institute of Technology Bombay (IIT Bombay or IITB) is a public research
university and technical institute in Powai, Mumbai, Maharashtra. It was established in
1958 as the second IIT to be founded, with support from UNESCO and funding from the
Soviet Union. IIT Bombay was designated an Institution of Eminence in 2018. Its motto is
"Jnanam paramam dhyeyam" (Knowledge is the Supreme Goal).

Campus
The campus spans about 545 acres between Powai Lake and Vihar Lake, roughly 15 km from
Mumbai's international airport, and borders Sanjay Gandhi National Park (which is why
leopards and crocodiles are occasionally sighted). The institute has 21 hostels; hostels
7, 10, 11, and 15, and a wing of the newer hostel 16, are for female students. Facilities
include two swimming pools; football, hockey, and cricket grounds; tennis, basketball,
squash, and volleyball courts; and a Students' Activity Centre (SAC) for cultural and
extracurricular activities.

Academic structure
IIT Bombay has 17 academic departments, 35 additional academic centres, and three
schools. Departments include Aerospace Engineering, Biosciences and Bioengineering,
Chemical Engineering, Chemistry, Civil Engineering, Computer Science & Engineering, Earth
Sciences, Economics, Electrical Engineering, Energy Science and Engineering, Environmental
Science and Engineering, Humanities & Social Science, Industrial Engineering & Operations
Research, Mathematics, Mechanical Engineering, Metallurgical Engineering & Materials
Science, and Physics. Schools include the IDC School of Design, the Shailesh J. Mehta
School of Management (SJMSoM), and the Desai Sethi School of Entrepreneurship.

The Academic Office facilitates and coordinates teaching and assessment, and maintains the
grades and academic records of all current and former students. The Senate, chaired by the
Director, is the institute's highest academic body.

Programmes and admissions
At the undergraduate level IIT Bombay offers B.Tech, B.Sc, and B.Design programmes; the
B.Tech programme is its most competitive. At the postgraduate level it offers M.Tech,
MS by Research, M.Sc, M.Design, and MBA, plus PhD and dual-degree programmes. Undergraduate
students are admitted through JEE (Advanced); postgraduate students through JAM and GATE.
The institute has more than 13,000 students and over 700 faculty members, and awards
degrees to more than a thousand students each year.

Governance
IIT Bombay is governed by a Board of Governors whose chairman is nominated by the President
of India. The Director, appointed by the Union Minister of Education, is the executive head
and chairs the Senate; the Registrar acts as secretary of the Board. The Director and two
Deputy Directors are supported by seven Deans and the heads of departments, centres, and
schools.

Student life, Gymkhana, and festivals
The Student Gymkhana is the overarching body for all student councils and committees that
coordinate student activities and organise events and festivals through the year. IIT
Bombay hosts major student festivals including Mood Indigo (cultural) and Techfest
(technical), among the largest of their kind in Asia.

Rankings
IIT Bombay was ranked 118th–129th in the QS World University Rankings (2025) and 1st in the
QS India Rankings 2025. In the NIRF 2024 rankings it was 3rd overall, 3rd among engineering
colleges, 4th among research institutions, and 10th among management schools.
